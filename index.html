Challenges and patterns

Hi! I am Melanie and I am interdisciplinary scientist/developer/artist, with a passion for technology, bioinformatics and various media of art and design. Originally from Vienna, Austria, I am currently living in Helsinki, Finland and completing my masters degree in Life Science Informatics at the University of Helsinki. I like challenges of all sorts (in my freetime you will find me climbing up big rocks) and learning new things and I have come a long way from completing my diploma degree in multimedia in Vienna to working as a data analyst in cancer research in Helsinki. I write code and poetry, I love finding patterns in everything around me, and I like to tell stories through illustration, photography, video editing, and design. I am most happy outside and one of my personal challenges is how to combine this love for movement and nature with science and technology.

2013

I graduated from high school with the highest success grade and a specialization on biology. During the graduation year I was part of the core organising team of River Jump! ,a volunteer-based environmental project funded by the EU, to bring together young people from 3 countries and learn about sustainability, nature conservation and activism in a festival format. River Jump! would continue to take place in various forms over the next three years. It was my first venture into organising bigger projects that required weekly meetings and continous effort, networking, grant applications and team communication.

SCI Austria was an important part of my life for some years around that time. Apart from River Jump!, I helped organise some volunteer workcamps, participated and video-documented one as part of an internship for SCI in Finland and was the workcamp leader of an environmental workcamp in Austria in cooperation with the Naturschutzbund Nieder√∂sterreich. I also made a video documentation on the 'Wikipedia For Peace' workcamp taking place in Vienna in Cooperation with Wikipedia and Wikimedia. Moreover I took part in some of SCIs training courses, such as the "Training for Trainers" course in Antwerp, Belgium and a 'Sustainable Living' training Poland.

2013-2015

After graduating from school I went straight on to study applied Multimedia at the Graphische in Vienna. Photography, Illustration, Video-editing, had always been a consistent part of my life and I wanted to explore it more professionally. I learned my way around the Adobe Creative Suite, around studio photography and digital illustration, design, film and audio. It was a bit of everything, but the thing I enjoyed the most was web-development, which was completely new to me. In our diploma project my team explored various problems around high-rents and abandoned buildings in Vienna, as well as alternative living situations and I built a website to showcase the information we collected. I even moved into the Wohnprojekt Wien, a sociocratically organised, community-living project, to collect some first-hand experience on the topic.
